# Poem English titles — provenance

The English title shown for each poem (in `ghostty-shaders list poems`, the
gallery tooltips, and `collections/poems.titles`) was chosen to be the most
**recognizable published** English name. Where a poem has a clear canonical
title — an English Wikipedia article, or Witter Bynner's *The Jade Mountain* /
許淵沖 (Xu Yuanchong) in the standard *Three Hundred Tang Poems* anthologies —
that title (or a concise form of it) is used. Where several renderings compete
with no single dominant one (common for cí named by tune, and for less-
anthologized pieces), the most recognizable concise form is kept and marked
*no single standard*.

These are display labels, not full citations, so long anthology titles are
shortened. Classical poems have no single "official" English title; this table
records what was used and why.

is marked *no single standard* rather than asserted.
